I’m a third-year PhD candidate in the Laboratory for Perception, Action and Cognition (LPAC) at Penn State University, advised by Prof. Yanxi Liu and Prof. Robert Collins. My work sits at the intersection of geometry-aware vision, transformers, and biomechanics, with a focus on learning robust structure from images and videos.

Vanishing Point Detection Recurring Pattern Modeling Transformers for Vision RGB-D / Multimodal Capture Vision → Dynamics (Biomechanics)

Research

1) ConVexNet — Transformer-based Vanishing Point Detection
Learning vanishing points from image patches (and later integrating line structure) using multi-scale features and attention, with an emphasis on capturing long-range geometric regularities.

2) Recurring Pattern Capture for Geometric Understanding
Exploring recurrence as a cue for scene geometry (e.g., vanishing points, symmetry), and designing representations that surface recurrence in feature maps and attention.

3) Vision to Dynamics
Estimating foot pressure / stability-related quantities from vision and integrating with biomechanics pipelines, with applications to center of pressure, center of mass, and human stability.
