Skanda Bharadwaj

PhD Candidate • Computer Vision & Machine Learning • Penn State University

I’m a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Penn State University, advised by Prof. Yanxi Liu and Prof. Robert Collins at the Laboratory for Perception, Action, and Cognition (LPAC). My research explores vanishing point detection, recurring pattern discovery, and cross-modal learning for human stability using pose and foot pressure data. I also interned at Meta with the Video Ecosystem Ranking team. Previously, I interned as a Research Scientist Intern at SLB, where I worked on real-time segmentation pipelines for environmental monitoring.

I completed my Master’s in Computer Science at Penn State, focusing on motion estimation in ultrasound imagery using deep learning and Kalman filtering under Prof. Mohamed Almekkawy. Prior to graduate school, I worked for over three years at Continental as a Computer Vision Engineer, developing multi-object tracking and traffic sign recognition algorithms for ADAS systems in collaboration with global automotive OEMs.
